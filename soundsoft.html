Sound-Visualization and Oscillator Software

I took on this project during my time as a research assistant for the computer science and education departments at UVA. It was created under a NSF-STEM grant as a pair to a curriculum to enable rural students to better understand and visualize sound waves.

Sound Oscillator Example Video


The oscillator allows the user to manipulate the properties of a sine wave and visually and auditorally see the outcomes. Additionally, they can visualize and hear how two different sound waves interact with eachother by manipulating the second sine wave and observing and hearing the overlay effect.

Here you can observe the manipulating of the sine waves and the subsequent effect of how the are graphed and overlaid. You also see the user press play to listen to the sound the wave would create.


Sound Visualizer Example Video


The sound visualizer visually represents sine waves being read in real time from the user's selected audio input. The user can stop the recording at any moment and then go back through the graph to further analyze the sound wave.

Here you can observe the user choosing their built-in mic as their input, then making noises and observing the resulting sine waves, and subsequently stopping the audio to further analyze the resulting sine wave.

Both programs were written in pure Python and packaged for deployment with PyInstaller.
