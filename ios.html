UVA Accessibility iOS App

I developed this app as my final project for my senior-level computer science course on mobile app development. UVA can be hard enough to navigate in and of itself, that I couldn't imagine how high the barrier to entry might feel for a student or visitor with a disability. Thus, this project began in the hopes of creating a resource for people at UVA in need of accessibility accomodations. It has has three main features: a GPS screen that shows the user's location on accessible pathways on Grounds, links to connect to SDAC resources, and a form to report any barrier to access.

Accessible GPS Screen


On the GPS screen the user can see their current location in relation to the accessibility maps that were overlayed on to the Google Maps platform. If this product were to be picked up for further development and deployment, I would suggest research into how to enable walking directions specific to the accesible pathways displayed on these maps.


SDAC Resources Screens


The SDAC resources were stored and pulled from a Firebase database that allows for resources added to be updated in the app in real time. Upon choosing a category the subcategories are presented in a new screen embedded in the navigation controller, where the user can click on their desired resource and be routed to the proper website.


Report Barrier to Access Screen


The Barrier Reporting screen allows the user to report any access issue they may have faced while on UVA facilities. An alertcontroller was used to verify proper and complete data entry. When the form is successfully submitted it will draft and send an email to facility management with the information provided by the user. Finally, the user's UVA affiliation and email are stored in a key-value pair to auto-fill for each additional report.
