Cultural Center WordPress Update

I spent the year after graduation working in a cultural center in Granada, Spain. Aside from being the most likely person on our team to make a major Spanish blunder, I also quickly became our team's go-to gal for all things tech. That eventually led to my updating the cultural center's WordPress site, which had been designed several years earlier and hadn't had a content update since.

Design Updates


As far as design went, we had three major needs: updated branding, activity content, and images. First, I updated the CSS so that the entire site reflected our brand colors, fonts, and logos. Then, I restructured the formatting to highlight our weekly schedules and social media.


Implementation and Documentation

On the end of functionality, we were facing two issues: we needed a way to link our public google calendar and needed form submission to go to the NGO's gmail. These were two easy fixes, taking advantage of Google's ability to embed both maps and calendars, and using WordPress's NinjaForms feature.


The main need however, was for documentation. I soon discovered that the reason the site had not been updated was that no one on staff knew how to do it. After making my edits, I made a step-by-step tutorial on how to update the content so that the website would live on past my time in Spain.


Find me, and the code for this website, on Github.
